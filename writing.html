The Survivors · Book One

Shattered Sails

Fantasy Adventure Found Family Grief Narrative Sea Voyage

In a world where isles graze the sky and empires are forged in promise, Lyla Vander is cursed. Bound to a magical amulet she doesn't understand, she flees the reach of the Hyllan Empire's most dangerous agents — the Inquisitors — across open, unforgiving seas.

Alongside four unlikely companions, she searches for answers. What unfolds is a story of loss, grief, and the slow, messy work of finding your place — structured around the five stages of grief, one arc at a time. Whether Lyla finds acceptance or demise depends on how far she's willing to go.

Chapter Excerpt — Shattered Sails

The cargo hold was cramped, the air damp, rife of rotted animal carcasses, and sweat. This brought a feral temperament to one Lyla Vander. Posing as Lynja of Karkova, she is riddled with anger. The ship turned her stomach into something fierce, every crash of the waves against the side of the ship caused it to tip to one side, and each tip caused her head to swell. With each bounce, Lyla grew annoyed. Every instance was another elbow to her side, another foot to her face.

The room itself was dimly lit, only a glow beetle illuminated the surroundings. Lyla groaned, she had enough. She made room around her easily and carefully got up from her confinement. She stepped over the bodies surrounding her, the Kizan who saved her, the dwarf who tricked her, an Otherblood Eif and Ma'i. Step by step, she tip-toed to the door at the end of the hold, pushing past large crates that seemed too light to move.

"Hey," a voice whispered out beside her, a large wooden object poked at her side. "What do you think you are doing?"

She stopped in her tracks, her face unmoved, yet a tad bit scared. She slowly turned around. The slender figure before her was draped in a grey robe that sunk to the floorboard, face hidden beneath the cloth — though two noticeable features were his pointed ears.

"Getting out? Breathing some air? I'm cramped in here," Lyla retorted nastily.

"You can't, not until they tell us. I'm sure your kind can wait a while longer," he spat, positioning himself closer.

"Excuse you!" Lyla scoffed. "Who cares, what can they do to us for wanting some air? And what do you even know, Eif?" The Eif moved forward, leaving no room, so close she could peek under his hood. A stone cold gaze shone back — harsh yellow eyes, fierce determination. She smirked.

"You Eifs like being cargo huh?" She spat at him. He flinched.

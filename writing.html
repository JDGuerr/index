The Survivors · Book One

Shattered Sails

Fantasy Adventure Found Family Grief Narrative Pirates

In a world where isles graze the sky and empires are forged in promise, Lyla Vander is cursed. Bound to a magical amulet she doesn't understand, she flees the reach of the Hyllan Empire's most dangerous agents — the Inquisitors — across open, unforgiving seas.

Alongside four unlikely companions, she searches for answers. What unfolds is a story of loss, grief, and the slow, messy work of finding your place, structured around the five stages of grief. Whether Lyla will find acceptance or demise, depending on how far she's willing to test her newfound power.

Chapter One — It's You, Lyla · Shattered Sails

Zaelar 3, 1085 PM; The Third Age — Cassan Province; Hall of Phisthae, Iomi Phar Atsa

All was quiet, all except for the gentle winds and waves crashing beneath the rock-infested sea beyond. It was all Lyla could hear. The morning chill clung to the bodies inside the courtyard, biting at the fingertips of the gathered prospects. The scent of raw industrial engines hung in the air alongside the low hum of the airships gliding through the skies. Lyla felt the calming, cool breeze, yet her focus lay elsewhere. Her vision tunneled, and all she could see was what lay before her. She noticed the flowing black tunic dragging in the wind, leaving her weight to the breeze. She felt at ease, a rush of determination. She raised her sword — a blade with ornate filigree along the hilt. Her mother's blade. Her knuckles whitened as she gripped the handle tighter. The man she faced readied his blade. She scanned him closely, igniting a spark in her eyes.

"BEGIN!" A voice in the far-off side of her vision shouted.

Her eyes darted quickly from one side to another. She closed both eyes, shutting them tight. Taking a long breath, poised and ready. Lyla lunged forward, thrusting her blade into her opponent. He parried her attempt, knocking the sword back to her. She staggered.

"Timing, Prospect," her opponent sang — she hated it, the fierce edge to his voice — "Timing is everything." His voice was carried only by a subtle warmth beneath it.

Lyla shifted her left foot forward, standing firm. She dug her left foot firmly into the dirt beneath, gripped the sword tightly and swung. With a quick twist of her wrist, Lyla swung once more, spinning on her heel as her blade came sweeping around in a low arc. Her opponent barely caught her strike, his sword met hers with a loud and heavy clang. The impact sent sparks flying, and shook Lyla's arm, sending a jolt down her spine. She held her ground, ultimately swinging her sword. Growing impatient with each counter, she attacked. With all her might she ran head on, prepared to strike a fatal blow.

Countered. He dodged out of her peripheral vision, knocking her off balance. "Remember, any head on attack can be displaced easily, weakening your resolve, Prospect."

Her footing faltered as she quickly blocked a swing from his blade. The opponent's grip tightened on his own hilt as he countered with a swift overhead slash. The force of his strike nearly buckling her knees. She pushed back, teeth gritted, forcing their locked blades apart with a grunt.

"Better." He grumbled as he thrust his blade forward, hitting her chest plate. "Bianc!"

She scowled, surprise twisted into a look of fury. Lyla lunged forward, her sword like flashes, as she delivered a flurry of thrusts, each driving him back as he attempted to put up an intense defense. This barrage sent him stumbling — he fell backward.

She looked over him, fury seeping through her untamable gaze. She aimed one final decisive strike and lunged at his chest. With brisk and fluid motion he reached his foot between her legs, catching her right leg and yanking it forward. She toppled over, hitting the ground hard.

Standing over her, he extended his sword with one hand, its tip pointed directly at her. With the other, he reached down, offering his hand to pull her back to her feet. She smirked. With a burst of strength, she pushed off the sword, kicking it out of the way with a swift kip-up. Her legs swung forward through the air, springing upright. Landing back on her feet in one fluid motion. Kicking her sword up into her hands she tossed it from left to right, readying her footing to strike. Spinning her wrist in a circular motion, Lyla spun her sword behind her back, the blade slicing through the air in a tight arc.

Her sword struck his shoulder. He winced. Lyla was indeed determined to kill. He immediately grabbed at her wrist. "Stop now, Lyla, let go."

"Stop it!" a voice rang out from beyond Lyla's sight. Her grip loosened, and the sword slipped from her hand, landing with a soft clang as it struck the dirt. She exhaled, annoyed.

"Prospect." The man who approached was draped from head to toe in white and black robes. Beneath them lay metal plates almost akin to armor. A porcelain mask adorned his face. "Is there reason for your costly behavior?"

"Lord Inquisitor," she carried that same annoyance over, "I was trained to always be ready, if I must land a fatal blow I shall, it is the requireme—"

"Prospect Vander, you speak as if we are savages!" his voice boomed. "We are not Kiza, nor Moarli, nor Orcs."

"I misspoke. Rather — I was prepared. I wouldn't have done it, Lord Inquisitor."

He returned his sight to Lyla, who confidently put on a poker face. "I don't doubt that, Prospect. Yet this is not your first chance at redemption." She dropped her face and shrugged. He contorted — disappointed. "Report to me once training ends."

Lyla sighed, casting a sharp glance at Cassus, who simply shrugged. With a defeated breath, she fell in step with the others, watching the Inquisitor's cape billow in the wind as he briskly stepped away.

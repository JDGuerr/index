Overview

A music festival planned entirely by students, for the community

Groovy GOAT — Growing Opportunities in Austin, Texas — was a full-scale outdoor music festival and fundraiser conceived, planned, and executed by 16 students enrolled in Dr. Nancy Salisbury's PR for Non-Profits course at St. Edward's University. Every element of the event — from venue logistics and band coordination to social media strategy and media outreach — was student-led.

The festival raised funds and awareness for two Austin nonprofits: Side by Side Kids, providing free after-school programs for K–5 students in underserved communities, and Fruitful Commons, a 501(c)(3) supporting community agriculture and food sovereignty across Central Texas.

Held on Andre Lawn at St. Edward's on Friday, November 8, 2024 from 2–7 PM, the event featured five live bands, 13 student organization tables, raffle prizes, and direct engagement with both nonprofits. Attendance was anticipated at 250–400 guests.

My Contribution

Publicity strategy — three channels, one message

As PR and Publicity Lead, I owned the external communications strategy for the festival — coordinating a multi-platform campaign designed to move an SEU student audience from awareness to attendance. My work spanned campus media, social media, and direct outreach.

📻
Topper Radio
Partnered with the university radio station and provided hosts with a scripted message to ensure consistent, accurate promotion. The station aired event details during peak student listening hours across multiple broadcast windows in the weeks before November 8th.
📰
Hilltop Views
Pitched the festival to the university newspaper as an editorial story, not just a calendar listing. Personalized outreach to writers and editors secured an interview, a feature article, and a published team photo — campus-wide editorial coverage money couldn't buy.
📢

Coordinated professor email outreach and 60–90 second in-class pitches via Fish Seminar faculty. Partnered with 13 student organizations to amplify through their channels. Designed and distributed flyers across dining halls, residence halls, and academic buildings.

Social Media Strategy

An AIDA-driven Instagram campaign

The Instagram strategy was structured around the AIDA model — moving the audience from first awareness to showing up on November 8th. Total output: 15 reels, 28 posts, 52 stories published from September 19th through the post-event recap.

A
Attention
Launch posts, promotional reels, and behind-the-scenes content of the team posting flyers on campus. Countdown stickers activated weeks out. Event details delivered in eye-catching visual formats from September 19th onward.
I
Interest
Performer spotlights and lineup reveals. Posts introducing the nonprofits and participating student orgs. Sustainability content for eco-conscious students. Community framing targeted toward freshmen navigating a new campus.
D
Desire
Giveaway post offering exclusive Groovy GOAT t-shirts to followers who commented and tagged friends. Escalating countdown posts building urgency as November 8th approached. Time-limited content creating FOMO.
A
Action
Every post included a clear CTA: share, tag friends, save the date. Real-time event-day stories captured live energy. Post-event recap reel published to highlight impact and build the brand for future editions.

Event Logistics

Venue, operations, and sustainability

  • Primary venue: Andre Lawn, St. Edward's University — open space with power access, stage positioned diagonally facing Trustee Hall, nonprofit and org tables in a semicircular formation around the lawn perimeter.
  • Rain contingency: Jones Auditorium reserved as a full indoor backup, with pre-planned layout for lobby tables, standard auditorium seating, and a greenroom for bands. Social media and flyers would update location instantly if needed.
  • Band coordination: Five performers confirmed with clear equipment requirements (bands provide own instruments; drum kit and mics provided), staggered sound-check arrivals one hour pre-set, curated Spotify playlist for set transitions.
  • Concessions and raffle: Hot dogs, soda, popcorn, and s'mores at a dedicated station. Raffle prizes included mini Bahama Bucks buckets with branded stickers, bracelets, and VIP gift cards. T-shirt giveaway run through Instagram.
  • Sustainability design: Exclusively compostable materials at concessions, zero single-use plastic. MC segments incorporated messaging on campus sustainability programs and Fruitful Commons' community agriculture work.
  • Staffing structure: Setup crew (1–3 hours early), event volunteers managing raffle and concessions, sound/tech team, and a post-event cleanup crew — all 16 team members assigned to specific roles.

Outcomes

What the campaign delivered

  • Campus newspaper feature: Personalized Hilltop Views pitch resulted in a published interview and article with a team photo — editorial coverage that reached the full student body organically.
  • Topper Radio presence: Scripted partnership with the university station reached students during peak listening hours across multiple broadcast windows in the weeks before the event.
  • 100+ nonprofit booth visitors: Both Side by Side Kids and Fruitful Commons tables saw strong traffic, with attendees engaging directly with representatives and their missions.
  • 13 student organizations participated: Multi-channel outreach successfully converted student orgs into co-promoters, each amplifying the event through their own audiences.
  • 95+ social media assets published: A full-funnel Instagram campaign (15 reels, 28 posts, 52 stories) sustained awareness from September through the post-event recap reel, building the brand for future editions.

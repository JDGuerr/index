Overview

Demystifying AI for enterprise audiences

FIXER PR is a student-led integrated communication agency operating through a Dell university partnership program. Our team was tasked with developing a full B2B marketing and PR campaign to position Dell's AI product line to medium and large organizations — addressing the gap between AI's potential and its adoption rate in the enterprise market.

Dell Technologies wanted to reach decision-makers at mid-to-large organizations who were curious about AI but lacked the clarity, confidence, or internal expertise to begin adoption. Our campaign goal was to demystify Dell's AI solutions — communicating their accessibility, adaptability, and long-term business value through a research-driven, human-centered strategy.

The campaign was built around a single insight: most businesses aren't avoiding AI out of disinterest — they're avoiding it out of confusion. Our strategy positioned Dell not just as a technology vendor but as a trusted guide through digital transformation.

Research Foundation

The numbers driving the strategy

Strategic Analysis

SWOT — Dell AI in the enterprise market

Our analysis revealed that Dell's AI has high potential for adoption but remains misunderstood among key B2B audiences.

S
Strengths
  • Reputation for reliability and security — positions Dell AI as safe and compliant
  • Fully customizable AI solutions adaptable to individual organizational needs
  • Diverse portfolio of industry partnerships (cybersecurity, software)
W
Weaknesses
  • AI remains confusing and intimidating — education is a major adoption hurdle
  • Offerings may read as too technical or corporate without simplified messaging
  • Mixed messaging across channels could reduce campaign clarity
O
Opportunities
  • Companies want AI but don't know where to start — Dell can offer setup, consultation, training
  • Growing pressure for sustainable AI creates "Green AI" leadership opportunity
  • Direct AI integration into Dell hardware for seamless enterprise deployment
T
Threats
  • Google Gemini, Microsoft, Lenovo, HP competing aggressively in enterprise AI
  • Risk of falling behind on chip design and AI accelerator advancements
  • Emerging AI privacy/security legislation could increase hardware costs

Deliverables

Creative Concepts

Four executions, one unified message

Each concept was designed to address a different moment in the B2B buyer journey — from awareness to education to trust-building.

01
B2B AI Print Adverts
Retro-modern print advertising targeting LinkedIn and B2B trade media. Two visual directions: bold typography-driven ("AI built for business? We got you.") and a heritage-nostalgia approach using vintage Dell computer aesthetics to contrast past and future.
02
Myth vs. Fact Seminars
Live and digital educational seminars directly addressing the most common misconceptions blocking enterprise AI adoption — reframing Dell AI as accessible, not intimidating. Designed to generate qualified B2B leads.
03
Demystify AI Video Series
A branded content series presenting AI adoption through real business scenarios. Tone: enlightening and empowering. Visual style: split-screen confusion-to-clarity. Light ambient synth scoring to signal progress without technical intimidation.
04
Website Redesign Concept
A redesigned Dell AI Solutions landing page built around three editorial pillars: "Why Dell AI?", "What We Deliver," and a human-centered visual hierarchy that educates first and sells second. Focused on reducing cognitive load for non-technical decision-makers.

PR Strategy

Channels, tools, and approach

  • Social media advertising — targeted B2B ads on LinkedIn, Facebook, and Instagram using platform metrics to track interaction and optimize spend.
  • Informative press releases — drafted to both announce campaign milestones and educate business journalists on Dell's AI capabilities — bridging PR and content marketing.
  • Webinars and online events — live programming designed to move prospects from awareness to consideration, with Q&A formats that directly address adoption barriers.
  • Creative and design infrastructure — brand guidelines, post templates, and presentation frameworks built in Adobe Creative Cloud and Canva for consistent execution at scale.
  • Budget strategy — cost-efficient B2B outlet targeting to maximize traffic and qualified lead generation within marketing budget constraints.

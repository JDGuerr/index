Overview

Working inside the Texas Senate

During the summer of 2023, I interned in the Austin district office of Senator Judith Zaffirini — one of the longest-serving and most prolific legislators in Texas Senate history. The internship placed me at the intersection of public service, strategic communication, and policy research.

My day-to-day responsibilities ranged from managing constituent casework and drafting official communications to conducting legislative research and supporting the senator's communications team with press and newsletter content.

Key Contributions

What I Did

  • Managed constituent cases end-to-end — logging inquiries, coordinating with state agencies, and following up with respectful, clear written communication.
  • Drafted press releases and newsletters distributed to Senator Zaffirini's constituents across District 21.
  • Wrote bill summaries and briefing memos for legislative staff, distilling complex policy language into accessible language.
  • Conducted background research on active legislation, policy issues, and current events relevant to the senator's committee work.
  • Supported day-to-day office operations during an active legislative period, collaborating with full-time staff under deadline pressure.
